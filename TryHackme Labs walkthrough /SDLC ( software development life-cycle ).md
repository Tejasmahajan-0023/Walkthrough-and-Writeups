**🌐Executive Summary**

The Software Development Lifecycle (SDLC) is a structured process that guides how software is planned, created, tested, deployed, and maintained. This TryHackMe room introduces learners to SDLC fundamentals, helping them understand each phase, key DevOps principles, and how these concepts translate into practical decision-making.

This walkthrough explains each task in the SDLC room, provides reasoning for every answer, and includes an in-depth discussion of the final challenge.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

**Task 2 Understanding SDLC?** 

Question 1 - How many phases can an SDLC have?
<details>
<summary>Reveal Answer</summary>

**6–8**

</details>

**Explanation** 

An SDLC typically has six main phases: Planning, Requirements Definition, Design and Prototyping, Development, Testing, and Deployment and Maintenance.

**Step 2 — Allocating Sprints Across SDLC Phases**

A practical sprint distribution could look like this:

Planning: 1 sprint

Requirements Definition: 10 sprints

Design and Prototyping: 5 sprints

Development: 1 sprint

Testing: 2 sprints

Deployment: 0 or 1 sprint

Maintenance: 0 sprints
